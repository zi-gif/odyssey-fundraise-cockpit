# Odyssey Investor Update: January 2026

Happy new year from the Odyssey team. January was a milestone month: we shipped Odyssey-2 Pro publicly and opened the developer API. The response from the developer community has exceeded our expectations.

## Key Metrics
- API signups: 1,240 (first 3 weeks post-launch)
- Active developers (weekly): 380
- Inference volume: 2.1M frames rendered
- Monthly burn: $620K
- Runway: 22 months at current burn

## Wins
- Odyssey-2 Pro launched January 8th. Coverage in TechCrunch, The Verge, and Wired.
- Developer API opened with streaming support at 720p / 22 FPS. Latency under 90ms p99.
- Signed initial enterprise pilot agreement with a major gaming studio (name under NDA). Expected to begin integration in February.
- Hired two senior ML researchers from DeepMind, bringing the research team to 12.

## In Progress
- Beginning partnership conversations with Nvidia around inference optimization on H100 clusters. Early but promising signal from their Developer Relations team.
- Targeting 5 total new ML researcher hires by end of Q2 to support next-gen model training.
- Working on Odyssey-3 architecture design. Early experiments on 1080p output show feasibility at 15 FPS.

## Challenges
- Inference costs are higher than modeled at launch. Optimizing GPU utilization is the top eng priority for February.
- Developer documentation needs significant work. We are getting support tickets that better docs would eliminate.

## Asks

- Referrals for a VP of Engineering hire. We need operational engineering leadership as the team scales past 20.
